Category 1

Masterful bronze castings from West Africa, 19th-20th century. Beyond their function as measures of gold, these Akan weights embody a profound artistry, where utility meets aesthetic mastery. Through their intricate forms, they preserve the legacy of a culture where craftsmanship, symbolism, and daily life was closely intertwined.

Product 1

Bronze serpent tightly looped in a vivid spiral with textured body and incrustated patina, showcasing Akhan mastery; serves as both measure and symbol of protection and eternal wisdom. Material: Copper alloy Provenance: Lucas Ratton Gallery Origin: Akhan people, Ivory Coast, 19th-20th century

Product 2

Exquisite bronze spoon, crafted not for use but as a symbol of wealth and artistry; a testament to the sophistication of Akan goldweight traditions. Material: Copper alloy Provenance: Lucas Ratton Gallery Origin: Akhan people, Ivory Coast, 19th-20th century

Product 3

Radiant sun motif cast in bronze, encircled by serpentine heads as sun rays, a symbolic fusion of solar vitality and protective forces in Akhan cosmology. Material: Copper alloy Provenance: Lucas Ratton Gallery Origin: Akhan people, Ivory Coast, 19th-20th century
